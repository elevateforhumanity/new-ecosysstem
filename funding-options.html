Funding Options & Eligibility

Many of our programs are funded through state or federal sources. When applicable, eligible students pay no tuition or reduced fees. Below is comprehensive information by funding type.

Important: Funding availability and eligibility requirements may change. Always verify current eligibility with our enrollment team before making program decisions.
üèõÔ∏è WIOA - Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act

Federal Workforce Development Funding

Coverage: 100% tuition and fees for eligible programs listed on INTraining/ETPL

Who Qualifies: Adults and dislocated workers meeting federal eligibility criteria

WIOA Eligibility Requirements:

  • Age 18 or older (Adult program) or meet dislocated worker criteria
  • Legal authorization to work in the United States
  • Selective Service registration (if applicable)
  • Meet income guidelines (for some Adult programs)
  • Demonstrate need for training to obtain/retain employment
  • Indiana residency or work in Indiana
  • Unable to obtain grant assistance from other sources

ETPL Status: Elevate for Humanity is an approved provider on Indiana's Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL)

Application Process: Contact your local WorkOne office or our enrollment team

üéØ WRG - Workforce Ready Grant

Indiana State Workforce Funding

Coverage: State-funded programs for high-demand careers

Who Qualifies: Indiana residents pursuing training in high-demand occupations

WRG Eligibility Requirements:

  • Indiana resident for at least 12 months
  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Demonstrate financial need
  • Enroll in eligible high-demand program
  • Commit to working in Indiana after completion
  • Meet academic prerequisites for chosen program

High-Demand Fields: Healthcare, Advanced Manufacturing, Information Technology, Transportation/Logistics

üîß Registered Apprenticeships

Earn-While-You-Learn Programs

Coverage: No out-of-pocket tuition costs while earning wages

Who Qualifies: Individuals accepted into registered apprenticeship programs

Apprenticeship Eligibility:

  • Age 18 or older (16+ for some programs)
  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Physical ability to perform job duties
  • Pass employer/union selection process
  • Drug screening and background check
  • Commitment to complete full program duration

Available Apprenticeships: Electrical (IBEW), Plumbing, HVAC, Advanced Manufacturing

Benefits: Progressive wage increases, health benefits, retirement plans, journeyman certification

üí≥ Self-Pay Options

Private Payment & Financing

For: Students not eligible for government funding or choosing private payment

Payment Options Available:

  • Full payment discount (5% off total tuition)
  • Monthly payment plans (0% interest for qualified applicants)
  • Employer sponsorship programs
  • Merit-based scholarships
  • Need-based financial assistance
  • Veterans benefits (GI Bill, VR&E)

Scholarship Opportunities: Up to $2,000 available based on academic merit and financial need

Military Education Benefits

Coverage: Various benefits available for eligible veterans and service members

Available Veterans Benefits:

How to Apply for Funding

Step 1: Determine Eligibility

Use our online eligibility checker or speak with an enrollment counselor to determine which funding options you qualify for.

Step 2: Gather Required Documents

  • Government-issued photo ID
  • Social Security card
  • Proof of residency (utility bill, lease agreement)
  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Income documentation (tax returns, pay stubs)
  • Unemployment documentation (if applicable)
  • Veterans DD-214 (if applicable)

Step 3: Complete Application

Submit applications through appropriate channels:

  • WIOA: Local WorkOne office
  • WRG: Indiana Commission for Higher Education
  • Apprenticeships: Employer/union sponsors
  • Veterans Benefits: VA Education Service
