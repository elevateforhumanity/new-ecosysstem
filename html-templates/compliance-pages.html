Funding & Eligibility

We proudly participate in multiple state and federal training initiatives. Many students qualify for fully funded training through established workforce development programs.

Available Funding Programs

WIOA (Workforce Innovation & Opportunity Act)

Coverage: Full tuition and fees

Eligibility: Low-income adults, dislocated workers, or individuals facing barriers to employment

Requirements:

  • Must be authorized by WorkOne Career Center
  • Meet income guidelines
  • Demonstrate need for training

Indiana Workforce Ready Grant (WRG)

Coverage: Full tuition and fees

Eligibility: Indiana residents pursuing high-demand career training

Requirements:

  • Indiana residency
  • Enrollment in eligible program
  • Complete FAFSA application

Registered Apprenticeships

Coverage: Earn while you learn - no tuition cost

Eligibility: Varies by employer and program

Benefits:

  • Paid training
  • Progressive wage increases
  • Guaranteed employment

Employer-Sponsored Training

Coverage: Full or partial funding

Eligibility: Current or prospective employees of participating employers

Process:

  • Employer identifies training need
  • Employee applies with employer approval
  • Training customized to employer requirements

Am I Eligible?

You may qualify for funding if you:

  • Are 18+ years old and legally authorized to work in the US
  • Are a resident of Indiana
  • Are unemployed, underemployed, or seeking career advancement
  • Can provide required documentation (ID, proof of income, education credentials)
  • Meet specific program prerequisites

Data Methodology

Data Collection

  • Employment Data: Collected through Indiana wage record matching and graduate surveys
  • Earnings Data: Based on quarterly wage records from Indiana Department of Workforce Development
  • Completion Rates: Calculated from students who complete all program requirements
  • Credential Rates: Based on students who earn industry-recognized credentials

Reporting Periods

  • Q2 (6 months): Employment and earnings data collected 6 months after program completion
  • Q4 (12 months): Employment and earnings data collected 12 months after program completion
  • Sample Size: Minimum of 10 graduates required for reporting

Data Privacy

All data is collected and reported in compliance with FERPA and state privacy requirements. Individual student information is never disclosed.

Accessibility Statement

Elevate for Humanity is committed to providing digital accessibility for all students, staff, and visitors. We strive to ensure our website and educational materials are accessible to people with disabilities.

Our Commitment

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards. These guidelines help make web content more accessible for people with disabilities and improve usability for all users.

Accessibility Features

  • Keyboard navigation support
  • Screen reader compatibility
  • High contrast color schemes
  • Resizable text up to 200%
  • Alternative text for images
  • Descriptive link text
  • Consistent navigation structure
  • Form labels and error identification

Student Accommodations

We provide reasonable accommodations for students with disabilities in our educational programs. Accommodations may include:

Learning Accommodations

  • Extended time for assignments and exams
  • Alternative format materials
  • Note-taking assistance
  • Audio recordings of lectures

Physical Accommodations

  • Accessible classroom locations
  • Assistive technology
  • Ergonomic seating arrangements
  • Modified equipment when needed

Communication Accommodations

  • Sign language interpreters
  • Real-time captioning
  • Written instructions
  • Alternative communication methods

Requesting Accommodations

Step 1: Contact Us

Reach out to our accessibility coordinator as early as possible, preferably before enrollment.

Step 2: Documentation

Provide documentation of your disability from a qualified professional (when applicable).

Step 3: Assessment

We'll work with you to determine appropriate accommodations for your specific needs.

Step 4: Implementation

Accommodations are implemented and monitored throughout your program.
