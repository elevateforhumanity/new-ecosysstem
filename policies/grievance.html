WIOA Grievance & Discrimination Complaints

Elevate for Humanity is committed to providing fair and accessible procedures for addressing grievances and discrimination complaints in accordance with federal regulations.

Programmatic (WIOA Title I) Grievances

Filing a WIOA Grievance

Who Can File: Any participant, applicant, or other interested party affected by WIOA Title I programs

What You Can Grieve:

  • Denial of eligibility for WIOA services
  • Denial of services or benefits
  • Quality or appropriateness of services received
  • Termination from WIOA programs
  • Other programmatic issues related to WIOA Title I

How to File:

  1. Submit your grievance in writing within the required timeframe
  2. Include your name, contact information, and detailed description of the issue
  3. Provide any supporting documentation
  4. Submit to our Grievance Officer (contact information below)

⏰ Important Timeline

We will issue a written Notice of Final Action within 90 days of your filing date.

If you do not receive a decision within 90 days, or you are dissatisfied with our decision, you may appeal to the state per 20 CFR 683 Subpart F.

Discrimination Complaints (WIOA § 188 / 29 CFR Part 38)

Filing a Discrimination Complaint

Protected Bases: Race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, childbirth, related medical conditions, sex stereotyping, transgender status, and gender identity), national origin (including limited English proficiency), age, disability, political affiliation or belief, citizenship status (for WIOA participants), or participation in WIOA programs

Filing Deadline: You must file within 180 days of the alleged discrimination

Complaint Process

What Happens After You File

  1. Acknowledgment: We will acknowledge receipt of your complaint within 5 business days
  2. Investigation: We will conduct a thorough and impartial investigation
  3. Resolution: We will work to resolve the matter fairly and promptly
  4. Final Action: You will receive written notice of our final action within required timeframes

Your Rights During the Process

  • Right to representation or assistance
  • Right to an interpreter if needed
  • Right to reasonable accommodations
  • Protection from retaliation

Retaliation Prohibited

It is against the law for Elevate for Humanity to retaliate against you because you:

  • Filed a complaint or grievance
  • Opposed a practice you believe violates nondiscrimination requirements
  • Testified, assisted, or participated in an investigation or hearing
  • Requested reasonable accommodations

Alternative Dispute Resolution

We encourage the use of alternative dispute resolution (ADR) methods, including mediation, where appropriate. ADR can provide a faster, less formal way to resolve disputes while preserving relationships.
