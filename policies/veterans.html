Priority of Service for Veterans and Eligible Spouses

Elevate for Humanity provides Priority of Service to veterans and eligible spouses for employment, training, and placement services in all qualified job training programs we operate, as required by federal law.

Covered persons receive priority over non-covered persons for access to services, resources, and opportunities in our WIOA-funded and other employment and training programs.

Who Qualifies for Priority of Service

Veterans

A person who served in the active military, naval, or air service and who was discharged or released under conditions other than dishonorable.

Eligible Spouses

The spouse of any of the following:

  • A veteran who died of a service-connected disability
  • A member of the Armed Forces on active duty who, at the time of application, is listed in one or more of the following categories and has been so listed for more than 90 days:
    • Missing in action
    • Captured in the line of duty by a hostile force
    • Forcibly detained or interned in the line of duty by a foreign government or power
  • A veteran who has a total disability resulting from a service-connected disability, as evaluated by the Department of Veterans Affairs
  • A veteran who died while a disability was in existence

How Priority of Service Works

Step 1: Self-Identification

During intake or application, please self-identify as a veteran or eligible spouse. You will be asked to provide documentation to verify your status.

Step 2: Documentation

Veterans: DD-214 (Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty) or other military discharge documentation

Eligible Spouses: Marriage certificate plus documentation establishing the veteran's status (death certificate, VA disability rating, military casualty report, etc.)

Step 3: Priority Processing

Once verified, you will receive priority consideration for:

  • Program enrollment when capacity is limited
  • Access to specialized services and resources
  • Job placement assistance and employer connections
  • Career counseling and support services

Services Covered by Priority of Service

  • Career Assessment and Counseling: Skills assessment, career exploration, and planning
  • Training Programs: All WIOA-funded and other employment training programs
  • Job Search Assistance: Resume writing, interview preparation, job matching
  • Placement Services: Direct referrals to employers and job opportunities
  • Support Services: Transportation assistance, childcare referrals, and other barriers removal
  • Follow-up Services: Post-employment support and retention services

How to Request Priority of Service

  1. Contact Us: Call (317) WORK (9675) or email veterans@elevateforhumanity.org
  2. Schedule Intake: We'll schedule a priority intake appointment within 48 hours
  3. Bring Documentation: Bring your DD-214 or other qualifying documentation
  4. Complete Assessment: We'll assess your skills, interests, and career goals
  5. Develop Plan: Create a personalized training and employment plan
  6. Begin Services: Start your priority services immediately
