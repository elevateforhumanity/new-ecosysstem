WIOA Grievances & Discrimination Complaints

Programmatic Grievances (WIOA Title I)

If you have concerns about WIOA Title I services, programs, or activities, you may file a grievance following these procedures:

Filing a Grievance

  • Time Limit: Grievances must be filed within one year of the alleged incident
  • Format: Submit a written grievance describing the issue and desired resolution
  • Where to File: Submit to our Grievance Officer at the contact information below

Resolution Process

  • We will acknowledge receipt of your grievance within 5 business days
  • We will investigate your concerns and attempt informal resolution
  • You will receive a written Notice of Final Action within 90 days of filing
  • If you are dissatisfied with our decision, you may appeal to the state within 60 days

State Appeal Process

If you do not receive a decision within 90 days, or you are dissatisfied with our final action, you may appeal to:

Discrimination Complaints (WIOA § 188 / 29 CFR Part 38)

If you believe you have experienced discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, political affiliation or belief, citizenship status, or participation in WIOA programs, you may file a complaint.

Filing Requirements

  • Time Limit: File within 180 days of the alleged discrimination
  • Where to File: With our Equal Opportunity Officer OR directly with the U.S. Department of Labor

Auxiliary Aids and Services

We provide auxiliary aids and services, as well as language assistance, free of charge to individuals with disabilities and those with limited English proficiency. Contact our Equal Opportunity Officer to request accommodations.

No Retaliation

We prohibit retaliation against any individual who files a grievance or discrimination complaint, participates in an investigation, or opposes discriminatory practices.
