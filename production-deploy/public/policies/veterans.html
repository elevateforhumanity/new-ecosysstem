Priority of Service for Veterans and Eligible Spouses

What is Priority of Service?

Priority of service means that covered persons receive priority over non-covered persons for the receipt of employment, training, and placement services provided under qualified job training programs. This priority applies to all services funded in whole or in part by the U.S. Department of Labor.

Who is Covered?

Veterans: A person who served in the active military, naval, or air service and who was discharged or released under conditions other than dishonorable.

Eligible Spouses:

  • Spouse of any veteran who died of a service-connected disability
  • Spouse of any member of the Armed Forces serving on active duty who, at the time of application, is listed as missing in action, captured in the line of duty, or forcibly detained by a foreign government or power
  • Spouse of any veteran who has a total disability resulting from a service-connected disability
  • Spouse of any veteran who died while a disability was in existence

How Priority of Service Works

Covered persons receive priority in the following areas:

  • Information and Intake: Priority access to program information and application processes
  • Assessment: Priority scheduling for skills assessments and career counseling
  • Training Services: Priority enrollment in training programs when capacity is limited
  • Job Placement: Priority referrals to employment opportunities
  • Support Services: Priority access to supportive services that enable participation

How to Request Priority of Service

  1. Self-Identify: Inform staff during your initial contact that you are a veteran or eligible spouse
  2. Provide Documentation: Present your DD-214 (veterans) or marriage certificate and veteran's documentation (eligible spouses)
  3. Complete Verification: Staff will verify your eligibility and document your priority status
  4. Receive Services: Priority will be applied to all applicable services throughout your participation

Documentation Requirements

For Veterans:

  • DD Form 214 (Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty)
  • OR other acceptable documentation of military service

For Eligible Spouses:

  • Marriage certificate or other proof of marriage
  • Veteran's DD-214 or documentation of service-connected disability/death
  • For missing/detained service members: official military documentation

Additional Veteran Services

In addition to priority of service, we provide:

  • Specialized career counseling for veterans
  • Assistance with translating military skills to civilian careers
  • Connections to veteran-specific resources and benefits
  • Coordination with VA education benefits and services
